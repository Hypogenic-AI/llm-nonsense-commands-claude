# Downloaded Papers

This directory contains PDF papers relevant to the research question: "Do LLMs Understand Nonsense Commands?"

## Paper List

### 1. Talking Nonsense: Probing Large Language Models' Understanding of Adversarial Gibberish Inputs
- **File**: `2404.17120_talking_nonsense_llm_gibberish.pdf`
- **Authors**: Valeriia Cherepanova, James Zou
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.17120
- **Why relevant**: **Most directly relevant paper**. Studies "LM Babel"—nonsensical prompts that compel LLMs to generate coherent responses. Investigates whether LLMs understand their own gibberish language.

### 2. Universal and Transferable Adversarial Attacks on Aligned Language Models (GCG)
- **File**: `2307.15043_universal_transferable_attacks.pdf`
- **Authors**: Andy Zou, Zifan Wang, Nicholas Carlini, Milad Nasr, J. Zico Kolter, Matt Fredrikson
- **Year**: 2023
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.15043
- **Why relevant**: Foundational paper introducing GCG attack. Demonstrates that gibberish suffixes can manipulate LLMs. Provides baseline method for generating nonsense prompts.

### 3. AutoDAN: Generating Stealthy Jailbreak Prompts on Aligned Large Language Models
- **File**: `2310.04451_autodan_stealthy_jailbreak.pdf`
- **Authors**: Xiaogeng Liu, Nan Xu, Muhao Chen, Chaowei Xiao
- **Year**: 2024 (ICLR 2024)
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.04451
- **Why relevant**: Shows contrast between GCG's gibberish attacks and readable jailbreak prompts. Demonstrates perplexity-based defense effectiveness against nonsense.

### 4. Jailbreaking Black Box Large Language Models in Twenty Queries (PAIR)
- **File**: `2310.08419_pair_jailbreaking_twenty_queries.pdf`
- **Authors**: Patrick Chao, Alexander Robey, Edgar Dobriban, et al.
- **Year**: 2023
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.08419
- **Why relevant**: Provides contrast with semantic (understandable) jailbreak prompts vs. nonsensical GCG prompts.

### 5. HarmBench: A Standardized Evaluation Framework for Automated Red Teaming and Robust Refusal
- **File**: `2402.04249_harmbench.pdf`
- **Authors**: Mantas Mazeika, Long Phan, Xuwang Yin, Andy Zou, et al.
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.04249
- **Why relevant**: Provides standardized evaluation framework and AdvBench dataset. Essential for reproducible experiments.

### 6. COLD-Attack: Jailbreaking LLMs with Stealthiness and Controllability
- **File**: `2402.08679_cold_attack.pdf`
- **Authors**: Xingang Guo, Fangxu Yu, Huan Zhang, Lianhui Qin, Bin Hu
- **Year**: 2024 (ICML 2024)
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.08679
- **Why relevant**: Shows spectrum from gibberish to fluent attacks. Demonstrates controllable attack generation.

### 7. JailbreakBench: An Open Robustness Benchmark for Jailbreaking Language Models
- **File**: `2404.01318_jailbreakbench.pdf`
- **Authors**: JailbreakBench Team
- **Year**: 2024 (NeurIPS 2024)
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.01318
- **Why relevant**: Additional benchmark dataset with 100 distinct misuse behaviors.

### 8. AutoDAN: Interpretable Gradient-Based Adversarial Attacks on Large Language Models
- **File**: `2310.15140_autodan_interpretable.pdf`
- **Authors**: Zhu et al.
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.15140
- **Why relevant**: Interpretable gradient-based attacks provide contrast to nonsense attacks. Useful for understanding attack mechanisms.
